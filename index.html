Project Overview

Based on the findings of a comprehensive feasibility study, this transformative project addressed the critical lack of awareness about National Research and Education Networks (NRENs) among Nepalese universities. Through strategic sensitization sessions, research domain identification, and exploration of global R&E services, the project successfully bridged Nepal's scientific infrastructure gap while expanding NREN's outreach to 7 out of 12 central universities and 1 provincial university.

Project Objectives

Primary Goal

Promote awareness and practical use of high-speed research and education networks (RENs) across Nepal's universities and research communities.

NREN Awareness Development

Develop comprehensive awareness materials tailored to the Nepalese academic context and research priorities.

Research Domain Identification

Identify key research domains across Nepal with highest potential to benefit from advanced networking infrastructure.

National Sensitization Campaign

Organize comprehensive sensitization workshops nationwide to build understanding and adoption of NREN services.

Team Capacity Building

Build team capacity through strategic international exposure visits to leading R&E centers and HPC facilities.

International Collaboration

Establish partnerships with global NRENs and research centers to expand Nepal's research capabilities.

Key Activities

Global NREN Services Research

Comprehensive mapping of activities, services, and resources of 42 global NRENs, including HPC facilities and collaborations, providing valuable insights into international best practices.

Nepal Research Area Mapping

Identified priority research areas across physical, biological, earth, computer, and health sciences through document review, consultations, and analysis of national trends.

International Capacity Building

NREN team visited HPC and R&E centers in China, Singapore, and Japan to understand infrastructure operations and potential collaborations for research advancement.

Sensitization Materials Development

Created comprehensive awareness materials tailored to the Nepalese context, incorporating global REN applications, research domain analyses, and practical benefits.

University Workshops

Conducted awareness sessions in 9 universities and 2 major APAN55 events, engaging 361 stakeholders and raising awareness about REN and supercomputing benefits.

International Exposure Visits

Strategic visits to leading research centers in China, Singapore, and Japan for knowledge transfer and collaboration development.

Challenges & Gaps

Limited Internet Infrastructure at Campuses

Universities outside Kathmandu lacked sufficient capacity for live demonstrations, limiting practical exposure during sensitization sessions.

University Coordination Delays

Scheduling conflicts with academic leadership caused postponements in some sensitization workshops, affecting project timeline efficiency.

Incomplete University Outreach

Sessions could not be conducted at Purbanchal, Nepal Sanskrit, and Lumbini Buddha Universities during the project timeline, leaving coverage gaps.

Future Directions

Complete University Coverage

Plan to visit and conduct comprehensive awareness activities at three uncovered universities to ensure complete national outreach and coverage.

Deepen HPC Collaborations

Strengthen partnerships with NSCC Singapore and China-based centers to expand access for Nepalese researchers across multiple disciplines.

Expand REN Coverage

Connect remaining universities to ensure equitable REN coverage and comprehensive national-level network integration across all provinces.

Enhance Local NREN Services

Use insights from global NRENs to introduce new services and foster advanced academic research capabilities within Nepal's institutions.
